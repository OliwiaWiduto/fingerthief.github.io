VENTRILOCAT

Brief / Playful Interaction

Role / UX, Physical Building

Software /Arduino IDE

Hardware /Arduino Uno, Adafruit Audio, Heart Rate Sensor

Ventrilocat is the answer to a cheap interactive technology using an Arduino system. It was designed to help users manage and communicate their mental health.

Brief completed in 4 months.

Research

Code / To understand the possibilities of Arduino, we spent 3 months completing software challenges, online tutorials & reading about the hardware.

Gadget / We took a hard look at the technological market to find there are many ways to keep track of physical health, but next to none when it comes to mental health. From there, we delved into wearables which focused on hedonism. Our project had never been made before, but with a little imagination we combined a basic health monitor with LED eyeshadow.



Process

Time / This project was extremely pushed for time therefore required a strict schedule with intense research & learning.

Problem Solving / Due to the steep learning curve, with 0 previous experience the project hit walls daily. We had to be extremely persistent and creative to overcome the constant challenges.

Sewing / I was able to show off my sewing & embroidery skills in making the casing for our wearable.

Reflection

Discovery / This was my first taste of university-level coding. Not only can I write basic code in Arduino as well as Processing, I’m also able to output it to LED and sound!

Improvements / To ensure it is in full working order, we had to keep this project very minimal. I would love nothing more than to develop it with an array of high tech sensors.

WITCH MUM SURVIVAL GUIDE

Brief / Animated Film Concept

Role / Sketches, Art Direction, Editing

Software / Photoshop, After Effects, Final Cut

Hardware / Wacom Tablet

“Witch Mum Survival Guide” is a concept for a 12 episode animated series. The idea for my mini-series was to create a show which would appeal to an entire family, creating a feeling of relativity and a means of reflection at the end of a long hard week for every member.

Brief completed in 2 months.

Research

Story / Having lived in Scotland for a month, the first step of this project was researching Scottish folklore. I concentrated on stories which were already slightly popular so I could build on my audience’s curiosity, but not so popular that I breaking down century old stereotypes, forcing my audience into cognitive dissonance.

Style / I studied various animation styles until I found the most fitting in terms of appeasing my target audience. I wanted realism but a style simple enough to be produced at low cost for distributors such as Adult Swim. As a family movie, I needed the show to carry strong morals and unravel like an allegory. Amy Sherman-Palladino, creator of cult show “Gilmore Girls”, names this as the success of her show.

Process

Art / Photos and sketches are key. For inspiration I used Pinterest, & the stunning architecture of Edinburgh. Its metropolitan tweaks helped me create a modern spin on ancient lore. The lead, Gaudy was designed to draw in mothers who would put the show on. The relatable b-list characters would entice the rest of the family to keep watching.

Reflection

Discovery / 1 week to draw 40 frames - the main skill I learned was speed drawing!

Improvements / In retrospect I wish I’d done this project in illustrator, as is the industry standard. I also wish I made better use of colour as a means of wayfinding.

POOL STORY

Brief / Playful Interaction

Role / UX, Physical Building

Software / Photoshop, After Effects

A 2 minute psychological drama about breaking through solipsism. The story is set in Dalry Swim Centre, located in the Merchiston area of Edinburgh. This piece is 2 dimensional, created fully in Photoshop & After Effects.

Brief completed within 4 months.

Research

Reference / The research aspect of this project was extremely hands on: I asked Dalry Swim Centre if I could come in and take photos to base sketches on.

Style / In figuring out the style, I compiled a moodboard on Pinterest and analysed movies of similar styles such as the works of Daniel Gies.

Process

Character Design / The high detail illustration meant endless hours of tedious drawing. It was very important to break down each character limb by limb so I could animate complicated movement such as swimming, using one drawing.

Animation / Similarly, I paid attention to the most intricate of details to give the characters’ movements realism which complemented the textured style.

Reflection

Discovery / How to professionally break characters down for industry standard animation.

Improvements / This project taught me I need more practice with walking cycles. Throughout my animation these looks unnatural, breaking the immersive illusion.

GARDEN SHARE

Brief / Practical Interaction

Role / Prototyping

Software / Axure, Pages

Garden Share is an app made to bring together small communities within cities, through sustainable gardening. It it designed for iPad, with the intent of promoting healthy living while social networking. The app is targeted at new adults, young parents, and retirees.

Brief completed within 4 months.

Research

Idea / To create my prototype, I studied sustainability in an attempt to understand its bare bones. Next, I gathered scholarly articles on sustainable technologies currently being researched, created and funded across the world.

App / To visualise the app, I carried out a 20-in-20 exercise, followed by a lot of sketching, colour & design studies.

Process

Mock Up / This prototype was fully made in Axure. I found the program to have a much steeper learning curve that InVision but rarely arrived at dead ends. With a lot of googling and processes which I was shocked to find hadn’t been automated yet, I mocked up Gardenshare within 1 month.

Feedback / To visualise the app, I carried out a 20-in-20 exercise, followed by a lot of sketching, colour & design studies.

Reflection

Discovery / I used Axure programme as UX researchers at Napier University had done substantial studies showing it is more beneficial to learn as compared with InVision. In retrospect, in terms of designing and showcasing I believe the latter is much better and will definitely be using it in the future.

Improvements / Too often my users were unaware of functions and didn’t know how to move on to the next step. Garden Share definitely needs critical way finding improvements through captions and buttons.
